Chapter 1 • Deep Learning with PyTorch

Introduction

A practical journey through modern deep learning with PyTorch—from first principles to real applications.

Deep learning has transformed how we work with data, from recognizing images and understanding language to powering recommendation engines and game-playing agents. This book is a practical journey through modern deep learning with PyTorch, designed to take you from beginner to advanced without requiring a heavy math background (though pointers to deeper theory are provided if you want them).

We start from the very beginning

We start from the very beginning: linear regression and the idea of a neuron as a simple nonlinear transformation of a linear model. From there, we build up to deep neural networks, then explore specialized architectures:

  • Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) for images and signals
  • Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs) and LSTMs for sequences and time series
  • Best practices for time series forecasting, including what commonly goes wrong in stock prediction tutorials

Why PyTorch

Along the way, you’ll see how PyTorch’s automatic differentiation and rich set of prebuilt layers let you focus on ideas and experiments instead of manually deriving and coding gradients. Compared to earlier libraries like Theano and TensorFlow, PyTorch emphasizes flexibility, clarity, and ease of debugging, making it a favorite for both research and real-world applications.

Practical applications

The second half of the book focuses on applications that matter in practice:

  • Natural Language Processing (NLP) and text classification
  • Embeddings for words and other categorical data
  • Recommender systems as used by companies like Amazon, Facebook, and Google
  • Transfer learning with pretrained models, allowing you to leverage massive datasets like ImageNet
  • Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) for realistic image generation
  • Reinforcement Learning (RL) for sequential decision-making and control

How we’ll work

Throughout, we will rely on PyTorch and GPU-backed environments (such as Google Colab), so you can train powerful models even if you don’t have specialized hardware. The goal is not just to show you which buttons to press, but to help you understand why the architectures work, where they fail, and how to adapt them to your own projects.

Bottom line: If you want to learn how to use one of the world’s fastest-growing deep learning libraries to build real, modern AI systems, this book is for you.

Part V — Deep Learning & LLMs

From the single neuron all the way to modern LLM alignment. Feed‑forward networks (concepts, then training/debugging), CNNs (theory then PyTorch), forecasting and RNNs, then two new chapters: Transformers & attention (the architectural bridge to LLMs) and modern LLM alignment beyond DPO.

Prerequisites Part IV end‑to‑end. You should be comfortable with the PyTorch training loop, autograd, loss functions, and the geometric view of a linear model before starting here.
How the chapters cluster
  • FFN track — Chapters 1, 2 (then optional Chapter 3, see below).
  • CNN track — Chapters 4, 5, 6, 7.
  • Sequence track — Chapters 8, 9, 10.
  • LLM track — Chapters 11, 12, 13.
About chapter 3 (optional / reference edition) Chapter 3 (“FFN canonical merged”) is a publication‑style merger of Chapters 1 and 2, kept verbatim from the original repo. If you read 1 and 2 in order, skip Chapter 3 — it repeats the same material. Use it as a single‑document reference if you ever want one long FFN article instead of the split lecture form.

About the LLM track (Ch. 11–13) Chapter 11 explains the Transformer architecture itself. Chapter 12 covers how to adapt a pretrained Transformer to your domain (SFT, LoRA, QLoRA) and the classical preference‑tuning step (DPO, RLHF) — this is the carried‑over reference text. Chapter 13 then picks up where Chapter 12 ends and walks through the post‑DPO landscape (ORPO, GRPO, KTO, RLAIF) with a decision matrix. There is intentional overlap on DPO between Ch. 12 and Ch. 13: Ch. 12 introduces it, Ch. 13 contrasts it.

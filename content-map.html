High-Level Content Map and Duplicate Ownership

This page is the book-level map created after scanning every chapter. Its job is not to remove repeated material. Repetition is kept where it teaches a concept from a different angle, but each repeated area now has an explicit owner chapter, a reason for the later revisit, and a suggested reading order.

Rule used for this correction pass No concept or factual detail is dropped. When the same idea appears in multiple places, the most complete chapter becomes the canonical explanation; nearby chapters are treated as introductions, applications, implementation views, enterprise views, or reference editions.

Book Spine

Part Primary purpose How to read it
Part I Enterprise AI delivery lifecycle: intake, kickoff, HLD, architecture review, LLD, sprint planning, provisioning, and scale. Read as a process story. Repeated roles, artifacts, and gates are intentional lifecycle continuity.
Part II Backend runtime, HTTP/FastAPI mechanics, authentication, identity, tenancy, workload identity, external integrations, and governance. Read in three blocks: runtime basics, auth identity, then tenancy/IAM/GCP implementation.

Part V Deep learning and LLM track: FFNs, CNNs, forecasting, RNNs, Transformers, fine-tuning, and alignment. Read by cluster: FFN, CNN, sequence models, then LLMs.
Part VI Supporting references: interview talk track and print-oriented classification source. Use as review material or a printable companion.

High-overlap pages Resolution

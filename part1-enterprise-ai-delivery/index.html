Part I — Enterprise AI Delivery Lifecycle

This part walks the entire lifecycle of an enterprise AI initiative — from a business unit raising a pain point, through the lead AI engineer's intake and response, kickoff, the seven days that follow, the High‑Level Design and its review, the LLD & sprint plan, and the provisioning realities of running on a real cloud platform.

Who this part is for Lead / Principal AI engineers, AI program managers, architects who need a reproducible playbook for getting from “we have a pain point” to “the pilot is in production.”
Prerequisites None for the lifecycle narrative itself. Chapters 6–7 (architecture, HLD/service selection) refer to FastAPI, OIDC, multi‑tenancy, and GCP — the relevant deep dives live in Part II. You can read this part end‑to‑end first and dip into Part II as needed.

Chapters in order

  1. From business pain point to AI lead — roles, BUs, the typical flow.
  2. The intake package — what should arrive on the lead AI engineer's desk on day 1.
  3. AI lead response document — exec summary, scope, metrics, controls, risks, asks.
  4. Pilot kickoff meeting — why, who, agenda, the 3 non‑negotiable decisions.
  5. Seven days after kickoff — day‑by‑day playbook + document pack.
  6. Architecture reference design — React, FastAPI, OIDC, multi‑tenancy.
  7. HLD & service selection — concrete GCP service choices, alternatives, enterprise delays.
  8. After HLD: next steps — what changes, what you produce, up‑managing.
  9. Architecture review — agenda, prep, capturing approval conditions.
  10. HLD → LLD & sprint plan — epics, vertical slice, story slicing, team shape.
  11. Provisioning, services, scale — tickets, latency, 1000s of users.
